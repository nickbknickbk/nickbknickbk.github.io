The Project Co-creating Interactivity and Performance across Cultures

A collaboration between Queen Mary University of London and Hunan University, Changsha, China, with the Kam ethnic minority, China. Over 13 days in rural China we built a Maker space in the village of Hengling, and co-created an interactive drama and interactive art exhibition which was attended by over 300 local people.

The Bigger Picture New Channel 2015 Summer Camp

Hunan University has led the New Channel Social Innovation Summer Camp successfully since 2009 sponsored by Markor Furnishings and collaborating with other Institutions such as HK PolyU (HongKong Polytechnic University), St. Martins (Central St. Martins College of Design, U.K). The participants of this summer camp have been not only students and professors, but also pro-fessional designers and social researchers and officers, and global companies like Microsoft and Huawei. The geographic location of camps are based in minority cultural areas in Hunan Province, China.

The aim of the Summer Camp 2015 was to undertake experimental action research in the area of Design and Society which can create new value for design and life through interactions between design and local culture and also local community, cultural remains, immaterial heritage and natural environment. The focus of 2015 is to help local people to realise the value of what they have and start the regeneration by themselves as well as with participants from outside.

TongDao County consists of Kam tribe villages - the Kam tribe is one of minority ethnic groups in China. The village is located southwest of Hunan province, and was selected as one of regions in-cluded in Chinaâ€™s grand project for inland regional regeneration. It is famous for its cultural heritages of architects, brocade and songs.

For 2015, QM led a team to explore the feasibility and practicality of creating and running a mobile makerspace for use in off-grid locations. To demonstrate the possible use of the makerspace we engaged local musicians and villagers in co-creating an interactive performance and interactive exhibition for local people.
